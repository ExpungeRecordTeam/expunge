FAQ

What is expungement?

Expungement refers to the process of sealing arrest and conviction records.

Who is eligible for expungement?

There are many factors that determine if your record is expungeable. This difficulty is why we created this site. By using this site you will be better able to determine if your case is expungeable.

Why is expungement important?

Having criminal charges on your record can prevent you from finding employment, public housing, and other government services. Expunging your criminal record can make accessing work, housing, and services easier.

What do I do if I don’t know all the facts about my criminal record?

If you do not know the exact charge, date, or outcome of your case, you can search your adult criminal record (including traffic violations) at WHERE PEOPLE CAN FIND THIS.

What if my criminal record is in a county besides Travis County Texas?

Our site can currently only direct you on how to expunge your criminal record in Travis County Texas. The expungement process differs from state to state and from county to county wihin some states, such as Texas. You’ll need to contact an attorney or legal aid service in the state or county where your criminal record is for more information.

Who do I contact if I have questions about this process?

Please contact a lawyer if you are in need of legal advice. A lawyer will be in the position to advise you as to what you should do and can aid in filing an order of nondisclosure.

If you have questions about the forms provided in our form section, please contact the Texas Office of Court Administration at (512) 463-1625.
